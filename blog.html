Is Cricket Really a Global Game?

✍️ Md. Shawon Masud 📅 2025 ⏱ 4 min read

Cricket has been one of the most fascinating games to many people in the world for a long time. It has evolved and thrived over the centuries. Standing on the national and international platform, has cricket actually managed to make a place in people's minds all over the world?

Mainly cricket is a skillful game. Nevertheless, the rules and processes of this game seem to be boring to many people which prevents it from gaining global recognition. We all know that cricket is much time-consuming game to watch. For this reason, the people of developed countries rarely watch this game as they are concerned about their time.

Being a global game, a football match takes only 90 minutes to complete whereas a cricket match of 20-over (T-20) takes around 3 hours and a 50-over (ODI) takes more than 7 hours to complete. So, it's obvious that fewer people will get time to watch cricket. It can cause obstruction for the World Cup not being able to go global.

Less team participation also suggests that this game is hardly going to be recognized globally. FIFA comprises 211 member nations, while the ICC, on the other hand, boasts just 12 full members along with numerous associate nations. The Cricket World Cup has often been criticized for its less team participation. For this, many countries are not getting chances to prove themselves on an international platform. If it continues, many country's people will lose their interest in watching cricket when their country will not be participating. It will lead the World Cup towards smaller audiences. This year, the ODI World Cup in India is running with just 10 teams.

"The World Cups are poorly designed, too long and with too few teams."

— Tim Wigmore, Deputy Cricket Correspondent, The Daily Telegraph

The Women's World Cups propound very few teams too. In 1973, the Women's World Cup started its journey with 7 teams. The 2022's Women's World Cup featured only 8 teams and it is again going to play with the same count in 2025.

"So allowing more teams is long overdue, though this year's ODI World Cup still only has 10 teams and the number in the Women's World Cup is only growing very slowly."

— Tim Wigmore

So, talking about the scope of cricket in the global platform, it is evident that the Cricket World Cup has been facing some major problems like losing audiences from developed countries because of time issues and also squeezing the global range allowing less team participation. Taking these factors into consideration, I personally think that the Cricket World Cup cannot be recognized as a global event. Cricket can still evolve and progress.

The Olympics has been a huge platform in the global market where a game connects with more audiences all over the world. Cricket has not been in the Olympics but it will be participating in the next Olympics at Los Angeles in 2028. So, it is a great opportunity to make cricket known to the global. But the chances of being acknowledged globally in the near term are slim.

If ICC works on solving its problem to make progress and be acceptable, only then it might be possible to make cricket recognized in the global platform.
